## Post Content

Jason Barnard shared this If you still think “topical authority” is what gets you recommended by AI, you’re only seeing half the picture. And in AI search, half the picture doesn’t get you chosen. Most entrepreneurs miss this: AI systems don’t just evaluate content. They choose between entities. So the real question is not: “Do I have enough content on this topic?” It’s: “Why would the system pick me over everyone else it already knows?” Topical authority gets you into the pool. Topical ownership gets you selected. And ownership is not one-dimensional. It quietly sits across a 3x3 reality: coverage, architecture, and position. At the point of selection, three things matter: ▪️Coverage — are you relevant and complete? ▪️Architecture — can the system understand you easily? ▪️Position — are you the entity it trusts most for this topic? Most brands optimize the first two. Very few intentionally build position. And yet that’s what decides the outcome. Because AI doesn’t compare pages at the recruitment stage. It compares entities. So if you’re not being recommended, the issue isn’t just content. It’s position. And position is what turns visibility into selection.
