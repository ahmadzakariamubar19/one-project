Harry Sanders shared this I've been doing SEO for over a decade and worked with now 1,000+ clients in my time running a global agency Here's my 8 predictions for where AI search is heading in 2026, and what I'm actually seeing play out across our client portfolio right now: 1. Traditional keyword tracking doesn't hold up when there are 2,000 variations of the same question, you need a full spectrum of tracking across keywords, prompts, and share of voice to see the full field 2. Searching through AI distributes your query across Google, Reddit, YouTube, and reviews before you even see the answer, so your brand needs to show up literally everywhere 3. Google's AI rollout follows revenue, they won't scale AI answers yet, we're almost halfway through 2026 and we've only seen snippets integrated 4. Parasite SEO, aged domains, hacking into LLMs, they work until they won't, and we've seen some of these shifts in this direction already 5. Traditional attribution is breaking, the buyer journey is now a spider web of journeys and consideration, teams will need to track revenue and demand 6. Brand mentions, media references, entity recognition are key to visibility, digital PR becomes a must do for brands looking to accelerate growth in search 7. AI visibility goes to who's known as a brand, not just who publishes the most content as everyone is cranking out low quality content 8. Agent-driven SEO workflows are here, humans augmented by AI producing better work, faster What's your hot take for the rest of the year?
