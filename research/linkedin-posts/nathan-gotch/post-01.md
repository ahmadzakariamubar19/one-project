Nathan Gotch shared this Most of the advice about “AI SEO” is overcomplicated nonsense. At the end of the day, the best way to get your brand visible in AI answers is still to do good SEO and focus on search everywhere optimization. That’s the game. If your brand has strong visibility across search surfaces and core retrieval sources, you’re far more likely to show up in AI answers. SEO is the lead domino. But I don’t just mean SEO on your website, even though that’s the right place to start. ➡ You should also be thinking about SEO on YouTube. ➡ You should be thinking about visibility across every important retrieval source. And the number one retrieval source is still search. Your brand needs to be present on the sources these systems are actually pulling from. I use Rankability to quickly find retrieval source gaps for any topic. These become your outreach targets. That gives you a clear path: increase your visibility on the retrieval sources that are already influencing AI answers. Do that consistently, and you give yourself a much better chance of showing up more often. There’s no real secret here. ➡ It’s consistent technical SEO on your website. ➡ It’s building topic authority on your website. ➡ It’s extending that authority across your other owned assets. ➡ And it’s earning visibility off-site on the sources that AI systems actually trust and retrieve from.
