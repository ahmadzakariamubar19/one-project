## Post Content

Chris Raulf shared this Still in Berlin with my wife, taking a few extra days to explore the city and a side trip to Prague. Incredible experience all around. But first, my highlights from Globalization and Localization Association (GALA) World Ready 2026 on April 13th. A huge thank you to Allison Ferch and the entire GALA team. And a special shoutout to Isabella Massardo , who invited me to do a GALA webinar shortly after ChatGPT launched. That single invitation changed the trajectory of my business. KEYNOTE: Deborah Nas set the perfect tone with her talk on the psychology of change. I loved the LEGO vs. Minecraft example and the question "What if books were invented after video games?" She also shared a quote by John Culkin: "We shape our tools, and then our tools shape us." A great reminder to be intentional about what we build. PEOPLE READY PANEL: "The AI-Ready Language Industry Professional" featured Olga Beregovaya , Viviana Bernabe, CPACC , Phil Le-Brun , Ana Vehar , and Rose Morgan . Big takeaway from Phil: most enterprises are still using AI for incremental efficiency gains rather than rethinking how they operate. The real premium now is on imagination and proximity to the customer. MY SESSION: I presented "From Search Engine to Answer Engine: Why Your Global Content Strategy Needs #GEO Now." The core message: to show up in Google AND large language models, you need Contextual Multi-Platform Content Creation, Optimization, and Distribution (CMTCC). The secret weapon is a virtual copy of you, built in Claude, trained on your expertise and voice. I shared the full prompting sequence in a blog post created specifically for this conference: [https://lnkd.in/ewhxVXKe](https://lnkd.in/ewhxVXKe) TECH READY: Kirti Vashee 's "The Death of TMS" was one of the most forward-thinking sessions of the day. His core argument: TM-centric systems are incompatible with how LLMs work because they fragment content into isolated segments. LLMs need context and document-level intelligence. His closing Steve Jobs quote really landed: AI should free humans from mundane work so they can focus on the beautiful stuff. CLOSING PLENARY: "What's in a Name? Redefining Localization for the Age of AI" sparked a great debate. A survey of 422 professionals found that "localization" no longer captures what most people in this industry actually do. The conversation around identity and value was both honest and necessary. SPEED NETWORKING: Met so many brilliant people from across the globe. One favorite moment: someone asked whether Virtual Chris is waterproof if he's going to advise me from the beach. Fair question. The opportunity is massive for those willing to think outside the box and embrace the tools available right now. It was wonderful to catch up with so many people in person, including Renate De La Paix , Belén Agulló García, Ph.D. , Martina Russo , Renato Beninatto , and so many more (out of characters).
